The Beauty of Simple Web Design

Posted: December 15, 2025 at 10:30 AM by Elvis Moraa

I've been reflecting on how web development has evolved over the years. While modern tools give us incredible power, there's something to be said about the elegance of a well-crafted static site. Fixed-width layouts, web-safe fonts, and simple color schemes made the web accessible to everyone.

This blog is a tribute to that era. No JavaScript frameworks, no CSS preprocessors, no bundlers. Just clean, semantic HTML and straightforward styling.

Building Systems That Scale

Posted: December 10, 2025 at 2:15 PM by Elvis Moraa

As a Machine Learning Engineer and Software Architect, I've learned that scalability isn't just about handling more users. It's about building systems that can evolve with changing requirements while maintaining reliability and performance.

At Pariti Inc, we scaled our platforms to serve over 100,000 users. The key was understanding the fundamentals: efficient data structures, proper caching strategies, asynchronous processing, and monitoring everything that matters.

The Power of Event-Driven Architecture

Posted: November 28, 2025 at 4:45 PM by Elvis Moraa

Event-driven systems changed how I think about software architecture. Instead of tight coupling between services, you have loosely coupled components that communicate through events.

Working with Kafka and Debezium at Pariti taught me the value of this approach. When services communicate asynchronously through events, you gain flexibility, resilience, and the ability to scale components independently.

The shift in mindset from request-response to event-driven thinking takes time, but once it clicks, you see opportunities for event-driven patterns everywhere.

Lessons from Building Production Systems

Posted: November 15, 2025 at 3:00 PM by Elvis Moraa

Production systems teach you lessons that no tutorial or course ever can. When your code serves real users with real expectations, you learn what matters and what doesn't.

I've learned to prioritize monitoring and observability. You can't fix what you can't see. I've learned the importance of graceful degradation. Systems will fail; plan for it. And I've learned that premature optimization is indeed the root of all evil.

Every incident, every bug, every performance issue is a learning opportunity. The best engineers aren't the ones who never make mistakes. They're the ones who learn from them and build better systems as a result.
